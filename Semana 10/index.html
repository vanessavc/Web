PROCESS

Lithoface is a modular experimental typeface made out of tiny rocks found in the floors of Altos De Chavón School of Design.The process involved carefully selecting and hand-arranging more than 1750 rocks to create all the characters. Next, I proceeded to photograph each character individually and transform them into vectors, trying to mantain their most important visual elements: shape and texture.

CONCEPT

With this typeface, I wanted to experiment with the idea of creating an organized structure based on organic elements. In order to represent this concept, I used a similar system to bitmap typefaces, but instead of using perfect dots, I used irregular rock shapes. At first glance, the characters look as if they were formed by dots, but later on we realize each module is actually different. Based on this inconsistency, I decided to create uppercase and lowercase characters to be used simultaneously.

In addition, since rocks are a very representative element of Chavón, I wanted to evoke the general mood of this setting by using a very specific color palette.
